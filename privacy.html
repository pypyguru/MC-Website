Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2024

Your privacy is important to us. It is Canadian Muslim Census' policy to respect your privacy and comply with any applicable law and regulation regarding any personal information we may collect about you, including across our website, and other sites we own and operate.

Personal information is any information about you that can be used to identify you. This includes information about you as a person (such as name, address, and date of birth), your devices, payment details, and even information about how you use a website or online service.

In the event our site contains links to third-party sites and services, please be aware that those sites and services have their own privacy policies. After following a link to any third-party content, you should read their posted privacy policy information about how they collect and use personal information. This Privacy Policy does not apply to any of your activities after you leave our site.

Information We Collect

Information we collect falls into one of two categories: "voluntarily provided" information and "automatically collected" information.

"Voluntarily provided" information refers to any information you knowingly and actively provide us when using or participating in any of our services and promotions.

"Automatically collected" information refers to any information automatically sent by your devices in the course of accessing our products and services.

Log Data

When you visit our website, our servers may automatically log the standard data provided by your web browser. This may include your device's Internet Protocol (IP) address, browser type and version, pages visited, time and date of your visit, time spent on each page, and other details about your visit.

If you encounter errors while using the site, we may collect technical details about the error, your device, and the circumstances of its occurrence. While this data may not be personally identifiable on its own, it could potentially be combined with other data to identify individuals.

Device Data

We may automatically collect data about your device when you interact with our services, including:

  • Device type
  • Operating system

Data collected depends on your device and software settings. Check your device manufacturer's policies to understand what information they share.

Personal Information

We may ask for personal information, such as your email address, when you:

  • Subscribe to newsletters
  • Contact us directly
  • Mention us on social media
  • Participate in surveys or promotions

Legitimate Reasons for Processing Your Personal Information

We only collect and use your personal information when necessary to provide our services, comply with laws, or for legitimate business interests (e.g., improving user experience, marketing, and legal compliance).

Security of Your Personal Information

We protect your personal information using commercially acceptable means to prevent loss, theft, or unauthorized access. However, no electronic transmission or storage method is 100% secure.

How Long We Keep Your Personal Information

We retain personal information only as long as needed for the purpose it was collected, or to comply with legal, accounting, or reporting obligations.

Children's Privacy

We do not survey children under 18, nor do we knowingly collect their personal information.

Disclosure to Third Parties

We may disclose personal information to:

  • Subsidiaries, affiliates, or service providers (e.g., IT, analytics, marketing)
  • Legal or regulatory authorities as required by law
  • Third parties in the event of a business transfer (e.g., acquisition)

Current third-party services include:

  • Google Analytics
  • Mailerlite

Your Rights Under Canadian Privacy Laws

  • Access/Correction: Request details of your personal information or correct inaccuracies.
  • Withdraw Consent: Opt out of communications or withdraw consent for data use.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your data, subject to legal obligations.
  • Complaints: Contact us with privacy concerns.

International Data Transfers

If transferring data outside Canada, we ensure protections through contractual clauses or other lawful mechanisms.

Cookies

We use cookies to improve your experience. For details, review our Cookie Policy.

Changes to This Policy

Updates will be posted on our website. Continued use of our services constitutes acceptance of changes.

Additional Disclosures for PIPEDA Compliance (Canada)

Under Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA):

  • We act as the data custodian for your information.
  • Legal bases for processing include consent, contractual necessity, legal obligations, and legitimate business interests.

For GDPR-related inquiries (if applicable to EU users), contact us for details.
